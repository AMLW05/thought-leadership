Artificial Intelligence (A.I.) Tools

The sheer number of A.I. tools available can be overwhelming. Here are a few current favorites curated for educators.

Brisk Boost

An AI-driven tool that provides real-time classroom insights and boosts student engagement.

Best for: Monitoring student engagement, tracking learning progress, and optimizing instruction.

Class Companion

An AI tool designed to provide feedback on student writing while encouraging revision and deeper thinking.

Best for: Assisting students with writing development, detecting AI-written content, and improving written responses.

Diffit

An AI-powered text simplification and differentiation tool for making content accessible to all learners.

Best for: Adapting learning materials to different reading levels and accessibility needs.

Gravity IO

An AI-powered visual collaboration platform for mapping ideas, planning projects, and managing workflows.

Best for: Project-based learning, team collaboration, and instructional design.

MagicSchool AI

An AI-powered platform designed to assist teachers with lesson planning, grading, and content generation.

Best for: Automating lesson planning, generating instructional materials, and streamlining grading.

Mirror Talk

An AI-driven speaking coach that helps students and educators refine their communication skills.

Best for: Improving public speaking, language fluency, and student communication skills.

Otter AI

AI-powered transcription software that converts speech into text in real-time.

Best for: Lecture transcription, accessibility support, and summarizing meetings or discussions.

Padlet

A digital bulletin board that allows for interactive collaboration, brainstorming, and resource sharing.

Best for: Collaborative brainstorming, student engagement, and sharing instructional resources.

Parlay Ideas

A discussion-based learning platform with AI integration that promotes critical thinking through structured student conversations.

Best for: Promoting structured discussions, student-led learning, and critical thinking.

School AI

An AI-powered teacher assistant that supports lesson planning, activity generation, and student progress tracking.

Best for: Automating repetitive tasks, creating personalized learning experiences, and generating curriculum-aligned quizzes and resources.

Snorkl

A video-based AI coaching tool that helps educators improve classroom instruction through self-reflection.

Best for: Helping teachers reflect on instructional strategies and improve classroom management.

STORM

A research prototype for interactive knowledge curation and AI-assisted writing.

Best for: Helping students synthesize diverse perspectives, generate research-based outlines, and refine pre-writing skills.

Fun Digital Tools

Random (but useful) tools that don’t fit well into the other categories!

Blackout Poetry Maker

Blackout poetry is made by coloring over parts of an existing text so that only selected words remain visible, creating a poem. Create blackout poetry with any text or use their samples.

ClassPad.net

An online calculator system designed for use by teachers and students in education — the online version of Casio’s ClassPad, without the need for the physical device.

Classroomscreen

All the classroom tools at your fingertips — timers, instructions, random selectors, and more. Use it face-to-face, digitally, or on an office wall monitor to share your schedule.

ClassTools.net

A website full of awesome digital tools. Favorites include: Hotspot Image Annotator (upload image, click to create hotspots, generate a shareable link), SMS Generator (create animated “chats” between historical or fictional characters), and the Twister Template (create fictional status updates — great for historical perspective-taking).

Factile

Create a Jeopardy-style game board in minutes. Factile is the #1 Jeopardy-style game creator for education.

Flippity.net

Easily turn a Google Sheet into more than a dozen interactive activities for free. Favorites: Board Games, Flashcards, Flexcards (Multimedia Flashcards), Manipulatives, Timeline, Virtual Breakouts.

Gzaas

Create full-screen messages for any occasion.

Hemingway Editor

An editing tool that helps users improve their writing. Writing is color-coded and labeled for readability issues, passive voice, adverbs, and complex sentences.

Kami

An online document annotation and markup tool. Highlight, underline, and strikethrough text in PDFs and other documents. Great for collaborative annotation.

Newspaper Snippet / Newspaper Generator

Create an authentic-looking newspaper clipping for free with Newspaper Snippet, or a full “fake” newspaper with Newspaper Generator.

PDFCandy

Modify PDFs for free. Every PDF tool you need is right here — merge, split, compress, convert, and more.

PhET Simulations

Online simulations for science and math from the University of Colorado at Boulder. Free, research-based simulations for interactive science and math learning.

The Differentiator

A great lesson planning tool for creating objectives related to Bloom’s Taxonomy. Generates differentiated objectives by cognitive level.

TimeToast

An easy-to-use timeline creator. Make a timeline, tell a story — discover the future, unearth the past.

Wakelet

A versatile curation tool. Educators can share lesson plans, create interactive study guides, manage classroom projects, and engage students with multimedia collections.

Wordclouds.com

A free online word cloud generator and tag cloud creator. Works on PC, tablet, or smartphone. Great for vocabulary, brainstorming, and visual summaries.

Gamification

These are just a few of the more popular game-creation tools. They have similar characteristics — try them all and pick your favorites!

Blooket

Fast-paced and highly competitive. Students love it! Multiple game modes with points, power-ups, and live competition.

Gimkit

A game show for the classroom that requires knowledge, collaboration, and strategy to win. Students earn in-game currency by answering correctly.

Kahoot

A formative assessment tool that can be used for team or individual game play. The classic live quiz platform — fast, familiar, and motivating.

Quizalize

A review game creation tool great for in-class or homework. Awesome for differentiation and tracking individual student mastery data.

Quizizz

A quiz game creation tool that can be played in the classroom or assigned for homework. AI-powered with tons of question types and meme feedback.

Quizlet

Study millions of study sets or create your own. Includes a live class game option, flashcards, practice tests, and learn mode.

Google Chrome Extensions

Explore the best Chrome extensions available for educators and students.

Better Canvas

Enhances the Canvas LMS experience by adding custom features, improved navigation, and layout customization.

Free Custom UI enhancements, improved navigation, and layout adjustments.

Note Only supports Canvas — does not enhance Blackboard or D2L Brightspace.

Brisk Teaching

A Chrome extension and web app that uses AI to generate lesson plans, automated feedback, and AI-enhanced grading.

Free Basic AI-powered lesson generation, limited assessment tools, Google Classroom integration.

Premium Advanced AI features, more customization, and expanded assessment capabilities.

Clip.me

A screenshot and screen recording extension. Capture, annotate, and share images or video clips directly from the browser.

Free Basic screenshot capture, annotations, and link sharing.

Diigo

A powerful bookmarking and annotation tool. Highlight web pages, add sticky notes, and organize resources for easy retrieval.

Free Basic bookmarking, highlighting, and sticky notes with limited storage.

Edpuzzle

Lets instructors embed interactive questions and notes into YouTube videos. Useful for self-paced video learning and flipped classrooms.

Google Keep

Saves highlighted text, images, and links directly to Google Keep for easy retrieval. Helps with research organization and note-taking.

Just Read

A customizable reader mode that removes ads, pop-ups, and distractions from web pages for a cleaner reading experience.

Free Basic reader mode with customization options.

Kami

A PDF and document annotation tool with real-time collaboration. Supports highlighting, underlining, drawing, and text tools.

Knowt

An AI-powered tool that automatically generates flashcards and quizzes from websites, PDFs, and notes.

Free Basic flashcard and quiz generation, note-saving, and Google Docs integration.

Loom

Record & share screen videos instantly. Great for tutorials, lesson walkthroughs, or giving detailed feedback on student work.

Mote

A voice commenting and feedback extension. Record and insert voice notes directly into Google Docs, Slides, Sheets, and Forms.

Free Up to 30 seconds per voice note, basic transcription, and Google tools integration.

OneTab

Converts all open tabs into a single list, reducing memory usage and keeping research or lesson planning organized.

Print Friendly PDF

Converts web pages into clean, printable PDFs, removing ads and unnecessary elements. Ideal for archiving resources and printing articles.

Read Aloud

Uses text-to-speech (TTS) to read aloud web pages, PDFs, and Google Docs. Supports multiple languages and voice options.

Free Basic TTS functionality with adjustable speeds.

Rememberry

A language learning and vocabulary builder extension that translates words and phrases on any webpage and adds them to flashcard review.

Free Basic translations, flashcard creation, and spaced repetition learning.

Tango

Automatically creates step-by-step guides as you perform tasks in your browser. Perfect for creating tutorials for students or documenting workflows.

Trancy

A language learning and subtitle extension providing dual subtitles, AI-generated transcripts, and instant translations for videos.

Free Basic dual subtitles, instant translations, and AI-generated transcripts.

Turn Off the Lights

Dims the background of web pages and videos, creating a theater-like experience. Great for reducing distraction when showing videos in class.

Free Full functionality including auto-dimming, custom opacity, and night mode.

Wakelet

Save web pages, articles, videos, and images directly to your Wakelet collections with one click. Ideal for curating and sharing resources.

Completely Free Unlimited collections and collaboration — no premium tier.

Workona

A tab and workspace organizer. Manage multiple projects, research sessions, and workflows by grouping related tabs into named workspaces.

Free Up to 5 workspaces, unlimited tab management, and session saving.

Google’s Extra Tools

A collection of Google’s extra tools and experimental sites that may surprise you!

Chrome Music Lab

Play with simple melodies to learn how music works. Great for exploring sound, rhythm, and composition in an accessible way.

Docs to Slides

A Chrome extension to send text from a Google Doc to a Google Slides presentation automatically.

GenType

Create one-of-a-kind, AI-generated alphabets from any idea. Type a concept and watch it become a custom font.

Google Arts & Culture

An online platform providing access to cultural artifacts, art, and history from institutions around the world. Includes virtual tours and street view of cultural landmarks.

Google Keep

A Chrome app and mobile app for quick note-taking. Notes can be color-coded and exported to Google Docs.

Google Labs

The home for Google’s AI Experiments — early-access experiments and tools from Google’s research teams.

Google Ngram

Examine the history and evolution of vocabulary and terminology in literature and recorded documents over time. Includes a “Getting Started” resource from the University of Pennsylvania.

Google Trends

See the topics people are — or aren’t — following, practically in real time. Also available: a TV screen grid of trending searches in real-time.

Note Be mindful of topics — some are for older audiences.

Google Trusted Tester Form

Interested in becoming a Google Trusted Tester? Fill out the form for a chance at selection for early access to new Google products.

Illuminate by Google

An experimental technology that uses AI to adapt web content to your learning preferences. Generates audio discussions from documents to make content more accessible.

Inside a Genius Mind

Dive into Leonardo Da Vinci’s personal notebooks — his codices — exploring themes across time and subject matter.

Learn About

An easier way to “Learn About” new topics at your own pace using conversational AI and Google’s knowledge graph.

MusicFX DJ

A generative AI tool that allows users to create music in real-time by inputting text prompts and mixing musical elements.

Music LM Workshop

Describe a musical idea and hear it come to life. An experimental AI tool that turns text descriptions into music.

NotebookLM

An AI-powered collaborator that helps you do your best thinking. Upload your documents and NotebookLM becomes an expert in your sources — can generate audio summaries.

Paint with Music

Turn your paint brush into musical instruments and compose on sensorial canvases. A creative intersection of visual art and sound.

Say What You See

Learn and practice the art of image prompting with quick games and challenges. Great for teaching AI literacy and prompt engineering.

TextFX

Enter a word and generate creative language possibilities. For rappers, writers, wordsmiths, and everyone in between — powered by Google’s AI.

Whisk

A Google Experiment to create a narrative with AI using images as prompts instead of text. Drag in images and generate new scenes.

Word Synth

A fun way to play with speech and music together. An interactive AI experiment from Google.

Learning Activities: Quizzes, Tests, Classwork, and Homework

These tools can be set up for automatic grading — don’t spend time grading multiple choice assignments. You can also use some of these tools to ask open-ended questions and grade them one at a time.

Actively Learn

An online literacy platform for ELA, Social Studies, and Science. Assign texts and videos with embedded questions, notes, and discussion prompts. Supports automatic and manual grading.

Answer Garden

A minimalist feedback tool. Doesn’t collect student identifiers, but allows students to submit short responses that aggregate into a live word cloud or list.

CommonLit

Assign and grade within the platform. The CommonLit Digital Library contains thousands of free, standards-aligned literary and informational texts with built-in assessments.

Decktoys

Create digital “breakouts” and/or learning paths for students to follow. Build complete lessons with activities, checks for understanding, and branching paths.

Desmos

Specifically dedicated to math engagement and real-world mathematics applications. Develop critical thinking through graphing, geometry, and interactive math activities.

Edpuzzle

Engagement you can see, results you can measure. Empower students to take an active role in their learning with interactive video assignments and auto-graded questions.

Figjam

A collaborative online whiteboard to brainstorm and collaborate, all in one place. Great for visual thinking, concept mapping, and group work.

Formative

A fantastic assessment platform that allows students to draw images, insert images, label pictures, and submit open-ended responses alongside auto-graded questions.

GeoGebra

A free mathematics application for geometry, graphing, and more. More than a set of free tools — a community of millions sharing math activities and resources.

Insert Learning

Keeps students engaged and active learners with interactive questions, discussions, videos, and live annotations layered on top of any web page.

Knowt

The #1 AI-powered alternative to Quizlet. Uses machine learning to automatically turn notes into flashcards, practice tests, and study guides.

Newsela

A comprehension platform with premium features. Pulls content from popular sites such as History.com and the Washington Post, with Lexile-leveled reading options.

Socrative

Effortlessly assess and engage your students while visualizing learning progress in real-time with instant results, quizzes, exit tickets, and space races.

Wizer

Create and assign “smart worksheets.” Add video, audio, images, and a variety of question types, all wrapped in a student-friendly digital format.
